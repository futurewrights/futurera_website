For parents

The questions you're already asking, answered honestly.

You are right to be careful. The internet is loud, the technology is moving quickly, and most "AI for kids" courses you'll find online are thin. Here's what we'll commit to — in plain language, without the marketing.

Why this matters now

AI is already reshaping the careers your child is heading into — in medicine, law, design, engineering, finance, journalism. Not a hypothetical future. Right now.

And schools haven't kept up. Most Canadian high schools have either banned AI outright or reduced it to "do not use it on essays." That leaves your child to figure it out alone, usually badly. Two thirds of Canadian students who use AI report learning less as a result — because no one taught them how.

By the time your child applies to university, every applicant will claim they "use AI." Almost none will be able to explain how it actually works or show something real they built with it. That gap will compound across their entire career.

This isn't just for "tech kids." Using AI well — with judgment, ethics, and limits — is becoming a baseline skill for every field your child might enter. The right time to learn it is in high school, with guidance.

What we'll commit to

Your child will leave Future Wrights with a real portfolio — projects you can hand to a university admissions officer or a co-op interviewer and have a substantive conversation about. Not a certificate that means nothing.

Their instructor will be a qualified educator paired with a working practitioner — not a generic tutor following a script. All instructors are police-checked. We'll introduce you before you commit.

The curriculum is aligned with Ontario Ministry expectations, so it complements school rather than competing with it. Cohort sizes are kept small so every student gets weekly 1:1 feedback from their mentor.

If your child isn't a "tech kid," that's fine — most of our students aren't. Our programs are built around using AI well in any subject, not just computer science.

We'll tell you honestly whether your child is ready for our program now, in six months, or whether something else is the right fit.
