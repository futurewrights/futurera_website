Three paths, depending on what you want

Everyone starts the same way: in AI Lab — Futurera's foundation program. From there, you pick what fits your goals.

Start here

AI Lab

The foundation. Build small AI projects end-to-end. Learn to think with AI, not just type prompts at it. Multiple courses, multiple formats — find what fits your schedule.

Academic path

University Track

Aiming at competitive CS, Data, or Health Tech programs? You'll leave with a real portfolio and a research artifact you can talk about in an admissions interview.

Industry path

Industry Track

More interested in working — internships, real teams, shipping things? Industry Track preps you for the interviews and the day-one realities of a junior role.

The apex (paid)

AI Studio — where we pay students to ship real work

Top performers from University Track or Industry Track get invited into AI Studio. Futurera is the legal employer; the work is real client engagements brought in from external companies. You get paid (stipend, age 16+, WSIB-covered), you ship under a senior practitioner, and you mentor younger AI Lab students. Learn · Coach · Deliver.

What we won't do

  • Tell you AI is magic. It isn't. It has real limits and real failure modes — we'll teach you both.
  • Hand you a chatbot and call it learning. You'll build, write, and explain. The chatbot is a tool, not the lesson.
  • Promise you'll get into Waterloo. We can't promise that. We can give you the work that makes the application a real conversation.
  • Filter by whether you're a "coding kid." We care if you'll show up, ship something, and be honest about what you don't know yet.
We treat you like the working professional you're three years from being. Bring that energy and we'll match it.

How to start

The next step is a 20-minute info call. Bring a parent if you want — or come alone, your call. We'll talk through your goals, what you've already built (if anything), and which program fits. There's no high-pressure sales pitch and no auto-enroll. Genuinely.

If you're nervous about the call, send an email instead. We'll write back like a person, not a marketing template.

If you're looking at Build with AI — From Prompt to Product

This is the first AI Lab course we run. If you're considering the bootcamp version (a 2-week intensive in summer), here's the case for it — in your voice, not your parent's.

Why you should take this course

You already know how to ask ChatGPT a question. Everyone in your school does. That's not a skill anymore. It's a baseline, like knowing how to Google.

What is a skill is being able to build with AI. That means writing the kind of instructions that get AI to do what you want on the first try, knowing what's happening under the chat window, knowing when an AI is lying to you, and putting a working AI tool at a public URL your friends can open on their phones.

Almost nobody in your grade can do any of this yet. Most teachers can't either. Two weeks in this course puts you in a category of person that companies, colleges, and the rest of the internet are going to keep paying attention to for the rest of your life.

Why this matters right now

The four ideas you learn here are the same four ideas the engineers at OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google work with every single day. The main difference between you and them at the end of two weeks is that they write production code and you work mostly with no-code and low-code tools. The concepts are identical.

In five years, "I use AI" will mean nothing. Everyone will use it. The people who actually go somewhere will be the people who can explain how it works and build their own version of it. That second category starts here, at age 14 to 18.

When you apply to college in two or three years, you'll be competing against people who claim they "used AI for an essay." You'll be the one with two live URLs to attach to your application.

What you'll actually do

You design the personality of your chatbot. You pick the subject your Study Buddy is built around. You decide what it sounds like, what it refuses to answer, what its name is, what its first message says. The work is yours.

There's a stress-test day where you spend the whole session trying to break your friends' chatbots on purpose, and they spend it trying to break yours. You document where the cracks are. This is competitive, funny, and the actual lesson at the same time.

On the final day, your parents come in and test your Study Buddy on your real homework in front of you. The room is loud. There are stakes.

Why you'll actually enjoy this

Most school projects end up in the trash on the last day of class. This one ends up at a URL that lives on the public internet for as long as you want it to. Your friends can open it on their phones. Your siblings can use it. You can keep using it after camp ends.

The two weeks are spent playing with the best AI tools that exist right now, in a structured way, with someone in the room who knows how to use them. You're not figuring this out alone in your bedroom at midnight.

You design what you build. You break your friends' work on a Friday. You walk out with two live URLs that work. That's a different kind of summer.
