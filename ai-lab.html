Program · Foundation

AI Lab — practical fluency, for any high schooler.

The foundation program every Futurera student starts with. Hands-on, ethics-first, and built around real AI projects students design and ship end-to-end. The goal is fluency, not familiarity — students leave understanding how AI works, not just how to type prompts at it. Open to any high schooler — no prior coding required.

AI Lab FAQ

Common questions about the program.

For questions about a specific cohort (dates, schedule, application), see the Summer 2026 Bootcamp page.

Is a coding background required?

No. AI Lab is built around using AI well in any subject — not just for students aiming at computer science. We assume no prior coding experience and rely on low-code and no-code tools throughout, with a mentor next to you the whole way.

What grade is it for?

Grade 9–12 (ages 14–18). Younger students (Grade 9–10) often benefit most from the bootcamp format — concentrated time with full mentor support. Older students (Grade 11–12) often pair it with University Track or Industry Track right after.

Online or in-person?

The Summer 2026 Bootcamp is in-person in Toronto. Future cohorts may include online or hybrid formats. We believe in-person delivery is materially better for the foundation stage, but we'll offer remote options where it broadens access.

Do students receive a certificate?

Yes — a certificate of completion signed by the lead instructor (a faculty member affiliated with UofT or TMU). But the more valuable outcome is the portfolio piece students build during the program. The certificate is the smallest part.

How is this different from a YouTube AI course?

Three things, specific to AI Lab. First, students do, they don't watch. Every cohort is built around real projects students design, build, and ship end-to-end, with feedback from senior faculty. Second, the curriculum is taught in real time by the same faculty who designed it, not a TA reading from a script. Third, AI Lab is a stepping stone into a full ecosystem — University Track, Industry Track, or AI Studio — so the foundation has somewhere to go.

What does it cost?

Pricing is being finalized and will be set per format. Get in touch and we'll share specifics as soon as they're ready. Need-based scholarships will be available, and we're pursuing IRAP and OCI funding to subsidize spots for partner schools and need-based students.
