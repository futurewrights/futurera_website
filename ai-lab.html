Stream 01 · Foundation

AI Lab — practical fluency, for any high schooler.

The foundation stream every Futurera student starts with. Hands-on, ethics-first, and built around real projects students design, build, and ship end-to-end — regardless of academic track or coding background.

See current cohort What students learn

Where every student starts.

Most "AI for high schoolers" courses teach how to type prompts at a chatbot. AI Lab goes further — students learn how the tools actually work, where they fail, how to use them well, and how to design, build, and ship real projects with them. The goal is fluency, not familiarity.

It's the foundation everything else in the Futurera ecosystem builds on. Students who continue into University Track use it as the base for research-grade projects. Students who continue into Industry Track use it as the base for internship-readiness work. AI Lab stands on its own too — a complete, valuable program even if a student never enrolls in another stream.

AI Lab FAQ

Common questions about the stream.

For questions about a specific cohort (dates, schedule, application), see the Summer 2026 Bootcamp page.

Does my child need a coding background?

No. AI Lab is built around using AI well in any subject — not just for students aiming at computer science. We assume zero coding experience and use no-code tools throughout, with a mentor next to you the whole way.

What grade is it for?

Grade 9–12 (ages 14–18). Younger students (Grade 9–10) often benefit most from the bootcamp format — concentrated time with full mentor support. Older students (Grade 11–12) often pair it with University Track or Industry Track right after.

Online or in-person?

The Summer 2026 Bootcamp is in-person in Toronto. Future cohorts may include online or hybrid formats. We believe in-person delivery is materially better for the foundation stage — but we'll offer remote options where it broadens access.

Will my child receive a certificate?

Yes — a certificate of completion signed by the lead instructor (a faculty member affiliated with UofT or TMU). But the more valuable outcome is the portfolio piece students build during the program. The certificate is the smallest part.

How is this different from a YouTube AI course?

Three things, specific to AI Lab. First, students do, they don't watch — every cohort is built around real projects students design, build, and ship end-to-end, with feedback from senior faculty. Second, the curriculum is taught in real time by the same faculty who designed it, not a TA reading from a script. Third, AI Lab is a stepping stone into a full ecosystem — University Track, Industry Track, or AI Studio — so the foundation has somewhere to go.

What does it cost?

Pricing varies by format and is discussed on the info call. Need-based scholarships are available — please ask. We are pursuing IRAP and OCI funding to subsidize spots for partner schools and need-based students.
