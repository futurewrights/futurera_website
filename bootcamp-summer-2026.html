What makes this cohort different

A small, in-person cohort, not a self-paced video.

Three things distinguish this bootcamp from the YouTube AI courses parents have seen advertised.

Small group

Real room, individual attention

Capped at about 15 students per cohort — not 5,000 names on a Zoom call. The instructor knows every student by name on Day 2. No back-row, no falling behind unnoticed.

Practitioners + faculty

Built by people who ship AI and teach at the university level

Curriculum designed by senior faculty on our core team — with appointments at the University of Toronto and Toronto Metropolitan University. Engineers and founders from industry bring current problems into the classroom. Graduate students from their labs assist in the room.

Real shipped work

Two live products, a real audience, real stakes

Students don't make slides about AI — they ship two real AI products to the public internet that friends can open on their phones. On the final day, parents arrive and test the Study Buddy on real homework in front of the room. Most "AI summer camps" don't ship anything.

Day-by-day

Week 1: Build the chatbot. Week 2: Build the Study Buddy.

Two real products, ten days, three hours each. Every student leaves with two live URLs, a 60-second pitch, and a one-page explainer they wrote themselves.

1

Week 1 — Custom AI character

Design and ship a chatbot with its own personality. Then break each other's bots on Friday.
Day 1 · Mon

System prompts vs. user prompts

The hidden layer that turns a generic chatbot into anything. Hands-on: the same task, phrased five ways, gets five answers.

Day 2 · Tue

Design your AI character

Pick a personality — a Bestie, a Sensei, a coach, an AP Chem tutor. Write the system prompt. Decide tone, name, first message.

Day 3 · Wed

Ship to a public URL

Deploy your chatbot to a real, shareable URL. Friends can open it on their phones the moment it's live.

Day 4 · Thu

Break each other's bots

The social day. Try to break your classmates' chatbots on purpose. Document the cracks. Patch your own.

Day 5 · Fri

Polish + reflect

Lock in v1 of the chatbot. Reflect on what worked. Set up Week 2 — the personalized Study Buddy.

2

Week 2 — Personalized Study Buddy

Build an AI tutor that knows your own school notes. Showcase it on Day 10.
Day 6 · Mon

Retrieval-augmented generation

What an embedding is, in plain English. Why ChatGPT can't answer questions about your homework, and how RAG fixes that.

Day 7 · Tue

Build the Study Buddy

Upload your own notes (PDFs, slides, docs). Build an AI tutor that answers from your material and quizzes you on it.

Day 8 · Wed

Hunt hallucinations

The same techniques production engineers use to catch AI lies. Stress-test your Study Buddy. Patch the gaps.

Day 9 · Thu

Write the explainer

One page, in your own words. Name the system prompt, the retrieval step, the model. This is what goes on a college application.

Day 10 · Fri

Showcase Day

60-second pitch + live demo. Parents arrive and test the Study Buddy on your real homework in front of the room.

What sticks

What stays with students long after the cohort ends.

The two live URLs are real, but the lasting value is what changes in the student — skills and habits that outlast any specific tool.

1

Confidence to ship something real

Most teens have never put a working tool on the public internet. After this, they have. The next thing they want to build is no longer abstract or scary — it's just one more iteration.

2

Vocabulary to explain AI without hand-waving

Embeddings, system prompts, hallucinations — in plain English. They become the student in the room who can answer "how does that actually work?" without bluffing.

3

A habit of stress-testing AI before trusting it

They've spent a day trying to break their classmates' bots on purpose. They'll never trust an AI's first answer again — a small judgment shift that protects them in school, on essays, and at work.

What we don't promise.

Every course on the internet over-promises. Here's what we won't claim.

What we won't claim

  • That students will become AI engineers in two weeks
  • That anyone will launch a startup off this
  • That the specific tools used today will be the same in a year — AI is moving fast and the software will keep changing

What we do promise

  • Students will learn the underlying skill — how to think about AI, design with it, test it, ship something real
  • That skill outlives any specific tool
  • That skill is what employers, colleges, and the world will keep paying for
Bootcamp FAQ
Is a coding background required?

No prior coding required. The bootcamp relies on no-code and low-code tools throughout — students write little to no code. The skills they learn (system prompts, retrieval-augmented generation, deployment) are conceptually identical to what working AI engineers do, just with a different interface. There's also an optional code track for code-curious students; both run side-by-side in the same room.

What do students need to bring?

A laptop. Any modern model works — Chromebook, Mac, Windows. That's it. All software is free and runs in the browser. If a laptop is a barrier, mention it when you get in touch; we can usually arrange a loaner for the bootcamp duration.

What tools are used?

All free, all publicly available: Lovable, Google NotebookLM, Claude, Bolt.new, Vercel. The deliberate choice is that students can keep using these tools at home after camp ends — the skills travel.

Students who opt into the optional code track pay a small $10–15 tools fee that covers API credits.

Where does it run? Can students from across the GTA attend?

Yes — students from across the GTA are welcome. Toronto, Mississauga, Brampton, Markham, Vaughan, Richmond Hill, Oakville, and beyond. We pick venues that are transit-accessible (subway and GO Transit), with a real classroom space — not a hotel ballroom.

The exact venue will be confirmed with applicants in May 2026 — typically central Toronto. tbd

What if a student misses a day?

We provide a recap and a 1:1 catch-up session with a mentor. The bootcamp is intensive — we strongly encourage full attendance, but life happens, and we don't penalize for one missed day. Missing more than two days is usually a sign the timing isn't right; we'd rather defer enrollment than rush.

Is parent attendance required or invited?

Not required. Parents are warmly invited to Showcase Day on the final day — students give a 60-second pitch and parents test the Study Buddy on real chemistry or history homework in front of the room. The day is loud. There are stakes. It's the moment that makes the whole two weeks make sense.

What's the application process?

A short written application (a few sentences about why the applicant wants to take it and what they'd hope to build), then a short conversation with our program lead. Decisions within two business days. Rolling intake until the cohort fills.

What does it cost?

Tuition is $1,499 CAD for the 2-week bootcamp — all materials and tools included. Payment is collected via a Stripe Payment Link after we accept your application. Need-based scholarships are available; mention this on your application or email us directly.

Refunds & cancellations

All cancellations and refunds are subject to the following policies:

  • All cancellations must be made in writing, via email, to Future Wrights Academy at admissions@futurewrights.ai.
  • Refunds, less an administrative fee ($100 CAD), will be issued for all cancellations made at least 30 days before your cohort's start date.
  • After the 30-day window, no refunds will be provided.
  • Pro-rated refunds or credits are not available for days absent.
  • Future Wrights Academy reserves the right to cancel a cohort if the minimum enrollment is not met or in the event of exceptional and/or unforeseen circumstances. If we have to cancel a cohort, we will accommodate the student in an alternate cohort, or provide a full refund.
Do you issue a certificate of completion?

We don't. Most short-form AI courses do, and most of those certificates aren't worth much. What students leave with — two live URLs, a 60-second pitch they've delivered, and a one-page explainer they wrote — is harder to fake and more useful in any college application, interview, or real-world setting.

If a verification record is needed for a school program or scholarship, we'll provide one on request, signed by the lead instructor.

What happens after the bootcamp?

The chatbot and Study Buddy each student builds keep running on their public URLs after the bootcamp ends — friends can use them, siblings can use them, students can keep iterating. Many continue using their Study Buddy through the school year for the subject they built it around.
