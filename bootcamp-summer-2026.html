Day-by-day

Build something new every day.
Launch your own app.

Ten sessions, three hours each. A real working project every day — and a capstone you design, build, and launch live by Demo Day. Every student leaves with a portfolio and a live URL.

1

Week 1 — Learn to build with AI

Day 1 · Mon

What AI really is

Run quick experiments on ChatGPT to see what it gets right, what it gets wrong, and what it gets confidently wrong. Walk out understanding the tool you're about to build with. Build today: your own AI "stress test."

Day 2 · Tue

Your new toolkit

Set up Cursor — the AI tool that turns plain-English descriptions into real apps — and build your first working pages. Build today: a live Birthday Countdown page.

Day 3 · Wed

The art of prompting

Learn what separates a frustrating AI session from a magical one, then go head-to-head in a live Prompt Battle. Build today: a reaction-time game.

Day 4 · Thu

From idea to instructions

Turn a vague idea into a clear, step-by-step plan before you build a single thing. Build today: a "How Well Do You Know Me?" quiz.

Day 5 · Fri

Thinking in components

Why does asking for a whole app at once make a mess? Learn to break a project into pieces and build it part by part. Build today: a clean, working Snake game.

2

Week 2 — Build and launch your own app

Day 6 · Mon

When the AI does the work

Discover that Cursor can search the web, run code, and generate data on its own — then learn the real-world skill that matters most: finding and fixing bugs without panicking. Build today: a Trivia game, built and debugged.

Day 7 · Tue

Teach the AI how you work

Stop repeating yourself. Use rules and skills to teach Cursor your style so every project gets faster and better. Build today: a "Which ___ Are You?" personality quiz.

Day 8 · Wed

Capstone kickoff + go live

No new concepts — just building. Start your own capstone project. The goal by end of session: a working version, deployed live, with a real URL. Build today: the first live version of your capstone.

Day 9 · Thu

Capstone sprint

A full focused build session. Push your capstone further — add features, fix bugs, and polish — with instructors circulating to help you get unstuck. Build today: a more complete, polished capstone.

Day 10 · Fri

Demo Day

Present your live app: what you built, why, what was hard, and what you'd do next. Parents arrive. Real demo, real audience, real applause. Walk away with: a deployed app, a presentation, and a portfolio piece.

Bootcamp FAQ
Is a coding background required?

No prior coding required. You build by describing what you want in plain English, and Cursor — the same AI tool professional developers use — turns it into a real, working app. The skills students practice (clear prompting, planning a build, breaking it into components, and finding and fixing bugs) are exactly what working builders do.

What do students need to bring?

A laptop. Any modern model works — Chromebook, Mac, Windows. That's it. All software is free and runs in the browser. If a laptop is a barrier, mention it when you get in touch; we can usually arrange a loaner for the bootcamp duration.

What tools are used?

The main tool is Cursor — the same AI builder professional developers use — alongside ChatGPT for early experiments. Everything runs on free tiers; the only optional cost is Cursor Pro (~$20/mo) for students who want more usage. The deliberate choice is that students can keep using these tools at home after camp ends — the skills travel.

Where does it run? Can students from across the GTA attend?

Yes — students from across the GTA are welcome. Toronto, Mississauga, Brampton, Markham, Vaughan, Richmond Hill, Oakville, and beyond. We pick venues that are transit-accessible (subway and GO Transit), with a real classroom space — not a hotel ballroom.

The exact venue will be confirmed with applicants in May 2026 — typically central Toronto. tbd

What if a student misses a day?

We provide a recap and a 1:1 catch-up session with a mentor. The bootcamp is intensive — we strongly encourage full attendance, but life happens, and we don't penalize for one missed day. Missing more than two days is usually a sign the timing isn't right; we'd rather defer enrollment than rush.

Is parent attendance required or invited?

Not required. Parents are warmly invited to Demo Day on the final day — students give a short pitch and parents try the app each student built, live, in front of the room. The day is loud. There are stakes. It's the moment that makes the whole two weeks make sense.

What's the application process?

A short written application (a few sentences about why the applicant wants to take it and what they'd hope to build), then a short conversation with our program lead. Decisions within two business days. Rolling intake until the cohort fills.

What does it cost?

Tuition is $1,699 CAD for the 2-week bootcamp — all materials and tools included. Payment is collected via a Stripe Payment Link after we accept your application. Need-based scholarships are available; mention this on your application or email us directly.

Refunds & cancellations

All cancellations and refunds are subject to the following policies:

  • All cancellations must be made in writing, via email, to Future Wrights Academy at admissions@futurewrights.ai.
  • Refunds, less an administrative fee ($100 CAD), will be issued for all cancellations made at least 30 days before your cohort's start date.
  • After the 30-day window, no refunds will be provided.
  • Pro-rated refunds or credits are not available for days absent.
  • Future Wrights Academy reserves the right to cancel a cohort if the minimum enrollment is not met or in the event of exceptional and/or unforeseen circumstances. If we have to cancel a cohort, we will accommodate the student in an alternate cohort, or provide a full refund.
Do you issue a certificate of completion?

We don't. Most short-form AI courses do, and most of those certificates aren't worth much. What students leave with — a deployed app on a real URL, a Demo Day presentation they've delivered, and a portfolio of projects — is harder to fake and more useful in any college application, interview, or real-world setting.

If a verification record is needed for a school program or scholarship, we'll provide one on request, signed by the lead instructor.

What happens after the bootcamp?

The apps each student builds keep running on their public URLs after the bootcamp ends — friends can use them, siblings can use them, students can keep iterating. Many keep building on their capstone or start new projects with the same tools through the school year.
