Is this the right fit?

We say no more often than we say yes — and that's the point.

Industry Track isn't an upgrade you buy. It's a checkpoint. Students earn entry through their work in Stream 01 or 02, and we're honest with families when the timing isn't right.

✓ A good fit if your student…

  • Has finished University Track or AI Lab with standout work
  • Wants real-world experience before applying to university
  • Is targeting competitive co-op programs (Waterloo, UofT, TMU, etc.)
  • Can commit consistent weekly hours — even a few — through the term
  • Is curious, accountable, and OK getting feedback in public

✕ Probably not yet if your student…

  • Is looking for a quick credential or résumé line
  • Hasn't done foundational project work yet (start with Stream 01 or 02)
  • Can't carve out a few hours weekly around school commitments
  • Wants a guarantee of an external job offer — that's not what we do
  • Is being pushed in by a parent without their own buy-in
How it works

Six concrete ways Industry Track prepares students for their first real role.

Industry Track is a readiness program, not the job itself. We close the gap between "I built a project in class" and "I can hold my own in a real interview, on a real team, doing real work."

1

Technical interview practice — done weekly, not crammed

The skill that decides most internships.

Mock interviews with mentors who've been on the hiring side at Canadian and global AI companies. Coding, system reasoning, and "explain it to a non-technical person" practice. Students learn to think out loud — the part most courses skip.

What it looks like: Weekly 30–45 minute mock interview with a working AI engineer. Honest feedback on what worked, what didn't, and one specific thing to improve before next week.
2

Real-world project simulation under mentor supervision

A safe place to make mistakes that matter.

Students take on a scoped problem that mirrors a real client engagement — requirements, milestones, a stakeholder check-in — but in a safe training environment with a mentor close by. The point is rehearsing professional behaviour, not pretending it's real work.

What it looks like: A 4-week simulated brief: build a small classifier, present milestones to a mock stakeholder, handle scope changes, deliver a final demo. Reviewed by a senior mentor.
3

Professional communication coaching

The most undertaught skill in any technical field.

How to write a clear standup update. How to ask a senior engineer a good question. How to disagree with a manager respectfully. How to give a five-minute demo. These are taught explicitly, not inferred from osmosis.

What it looks like: Weekly written and verbal exercises, with feedback. Students leave able to communicate like a junior professional, not like a kid showing schoolwork.
4

Mentor matching with industry practitioners

A real practitioner. A real conversation. Every week.

Each student is matched with an industry mentor — a working AI practitioner, often a hiring manager — who meets with them regularly. The relationship lasts beyond Industry Track and is one of the strongest things students leave with.

What it looks like: A 30-min mentor 1:1 each week. Career-relevant conversation, project review, and direct introductions when the time is right.
5

Demo Day showcase

Quarterly. Curated audience.

Each term we host a Demo Day where Industry Track students present their work to a curated room of startup founders, alumni, and faculty from UofT and TMU. It's the highest-leverage hour your student spends in the program — preparation collides with opportunity.

What it looks like: Your student gives a 5-minute demo to 30–40 people who all know what they're looking at. Conversations follow. Some lead to AI Studio invitations, internship offers, or research opportunities.
6

Fast-track to AI Studio (the paid program)

The on-ramp to real, paid work — for top performers.

Industry Track is the on-ramp. AI Studio is the paid destination. Top performers from Industry Track are invited into AI Studio engagements, where Futurera is the legal employer, the work is real client engagements, and students earn a stipend on qualifying engagements at age 16+.

What it looks like: A standout Industry Track student is offered a Studio engagement: 6–8 hours per week, paired with a senior practitioner, on a real client project. WSIB-covered, parental consent, age-appropriate scope. Paid.

Honest framing

What we promise — and what we won't.

Most education programs over-promise. We'd rather lose a sale than mislead a parent.

✓ What we will guarantee

  • Mentored, supervised work on real problems
  • A senior practitioner accountable for delivery on every project
  • Parental visibility — you can see what your student is doing and with whom
  • WSIB coverage on paid engagements; safe and legal at every step
  • Pathways into Ontario Co-op & SHSM where eligible
  • Honest feedback to your student about what's working and what isn't

✕ What we won't claim

  • That Industry Track itself pays students — it's a readiness program (paid work happens in AI Studio, by invitation)
  • That every Industry Track student is invited into AI Studio
  • That we'll get your 16-year-old hired at Shopify
  • That university admissions are guaranteed by anything we do
  • That every readiness exercise is "real client work" — most are simulations
  • That we'll keep a student in the program if they're not engaging
Mentors & oversight

How we keep students safe and the work serious.

Every Industry Track engagement runs on three commitments. These aren't aspirational — they're enforced before any student is staffed.

1. Senior accountable on every project

A practitioner with shipping experience owns the deliverable. The student contributes; the senior signs off. Clients know this.

2. Weekly 1:1 review

Every student has a 30-min check-in with their mentor each week. We review work, blockers, and wellbeing — not just deliverables.

3. No unsupervised client contact

Students never speak to a client without their mentor present. Not in meetings, not in email threads. Full stop.

FAQ

The questions parents ask first.

Are students paid in Industry Track?

No — Industry Track itself is a readiness program, not the paid work. Think of it as the training ground that prepares students for a real role.

The paid work happens in AI Studio, Futurera's apex program. Top performers from Industry Track (and from University Track, where the work is research-leaning) are invited into AI Studio engagements. AI Studio students earn a stipend on qualifying engagements at age 16+, with Futurera as the legal employer of record. We document everything via T4 / T4A so it's clean for tax time.

Are students legally protected if something goes wrong?

Yes. We require parent/guardian consent on enrollment. Mentors are present on every external client interaction (and most internal ones). We carry program liability insurance. AI Studio paid placements are WSIB-covered. And we run police-checked instructors — not optional.

How many hours per week is realistic?

4–8 hours per week is typical, scheduled around school. Some students do more during breaks; some do less during exam weeks. We design engagements to flex around academics, not the other way around.

Can this count as a high school co-op credit?

Yes — we are set up as an Ontario co-op employer and work directly with school co-op coordinators. If a student's school requires a co-op placement, we can structure their Industry Track engagement to satisfy that requirement.

We also support Specialist High Skills Major (SHSM) in ICT, where applicable.

What if a student applies to Industry Track and isn't ready?

They stay in AI Lab or University Track until their work is ready. There's no pressure, no extra fee, no penalty. We've turned away students with cheque-in-hand parents because the timing wasn't right — that's the standard.

Is this a guarantee of a job?

No, and we'll never claim that. Industry Track creates interview readiness and credible connections. What that turns into — an AI Studio invitation, an external internship, a co-op offer, a research opportunity — depends on your student, the market, and timing.

What we do guarantee: a polished portfolio, a mentor reference letter, interview-ready confidence, and a measurably stronger position when the real interviews come.

Can students work on a project of their own choosing?

For self-driven projects (their own startup idea, a personal research question), University Track is usually the right home — students get mentor time and structure to build their own thing. Industry Track is specifically about preparing for someone else's brief, under supervision, with feedback. Both are valuable; they teach different muscles.

What does it cost?

Industry Track does not have a separate enrollment fee for students who've completed AI Lab. Pricing for AI Lab and University Track is discussed on the info call. AI Studio engagements pay students (not the other way around) — qualifying engagements include a stipend at age 16+.
